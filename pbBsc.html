About the Programme

What is PB B.Sc Nursing?

The Post Basic B.Sc. Nursing (PB B.Sc. Nursing) is a two-year undergraduate programme designed for registered nurses who already hold a GNM diploma and want to upgrade their education and professional competence.

Recognized by the Indian Nursing Council (INC), the course builds on existing clinical experience and prepares graduates for senior nursing roles, nursing education, healthcare administration, and higher studies.

Programme Goals

Programme Objectives

Graduates of PB B.Sc. Nursing will be able to:

  • Upgrade their professional competence as bedside nurses, nurse educators, and administrators.
  • Provide comprehensive nursing care using advanced clinical knowledge.
  • Take up leadership and supervisory roles in healthcare organizations.
  • Promote community health and evidence-based nursing practices.
  • Pursue higher education such as M.Sc. Nursing, Nurse Practitioner programmes, or Ph.D.

Assessment

Examination & Evaluation

Assessment is based on internal evaluation and university examinations, with separate passing requirements for theory and practicals. Attendance requirements are also clearly defined.

Component Weightage Details
Internal Assessment 25% Assignments, seminars, and clinical performance
University Examination 75% Theory and practical examinations
Passing Criteria 50% Minimum 50% in theory and practicals separately
Theory Attendance 75% Minimum required to appear for exams
Clinical Attendance 100% Full attendance required in clinical training
