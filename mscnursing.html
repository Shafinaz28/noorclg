About the Programme

What is M.Sc Nursing?

The Master of Science in Nursing (M.Sc. Nursing) is a two-year postgraduate programme recognised by the Indian Nursing Council (INC) and State Nursing Council. It is designed to prepare graduate nurses for advanced clinical practice, teaching, administration, and research.

The programme focuses on developing specialised nursing professionals who can take up leadership roles in healthcare, contribute to nursing education, and engage in evidence-based research to improve patient outcomes and healthcare systems across India and globally.

Programme Goals

Programme Objectives

Graduates of the M.Sc. Nursing programme at Noor College will be equipped to lead, teach, research, and innovate across all dimensions of modern healthcare:

  • Deliver specialised nursing care in chosen areas of clinical expertise with advanced competence.
  • Function as nurse educators, administrators, researchers, and healthcare leaders.
  • Apply advanced clinical decision-making skills and evidence-based nursing practice.
  • Contribute to the development of nursing science through original research and dissertation.
  • Take up policy-making, teaching, and consultancy roles in healthcare institutions.
  • Pursue doctoral studies (Ph.D.) and global advanced practice nursing opportunities.

Research & Dissertation

Every M.Sc. Nursing student at Noor College must complete and submit an original research dissertation as a compulsory requirement for degree completion. Students are supported by experienced faculty with doctoral qualifications throughout the research process — from proposal to viva-voce.

Assessment

Assessment across four semesters combines internal continuous evaluation with university examinations and a compulsory dissertation viva-voce — ensuring graduates are evaluated at the highest academic and clinical standards.
