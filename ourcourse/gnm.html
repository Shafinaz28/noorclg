About the Programme

What is the GNM Programme?

The Diploma in General Nursing & Midwifery (GNM) is a three-year professional course designed to prepare students as skilled nurses and midwives. Recognized by the Indian Nursing Council (INC), the programme equips students with theoretical knowledge, clinical training, and hands-on experience to work in hospitals, clinics, and community healthcare centers.

At Noor College, we focus on holistic education — nurturing not only professional skills but also compassion, empathy, and ethical responsibility, making our graduates ready to serve society with excellence.

Programme Goals

Programme Objectives

Graduates of the GNM programme at Noor College will be equipped to:

  • Deliver safe and effective nursing care to patients across all settings.
  • Function as competent midwives — assisting mothers during pregnancy, childbirth, and postnatal care.
  • Provide preventive, promotive, curative, and rehabilitative health services.
  • Work as valuable members of healthcare teams in hospitals and communities.
  • Pursue higher education in nursing and related healthcare fields.

Assessment & Evaluation

Examination & Evaluation

Assessment is conducted through a combination of continuous internal evaluation and state board examinations, ensuring students are comprehensively evaluated on both theory and practical competence.

Component Weightage Details
Internal Assessment 25% Assignments, class tests, clinical performance records
Board Examination 75% Conducted by State Nursing Examination Board
Passing – Theory & Practicals 50% Minimum 50% required separately in theory and practicals
Passing – English 40% Minimum 40% required in English paper
Theory Attendance 75% Minimum attendance required to appear for board exams
Clinical Attendance 100% Full clinical attendance mandatory throughout the programme
Final Year

Internship Programme

Internship is an integral part of the GNM programme, forming the backbone of the final year. Students gain structured, supervised experience across five key clinical departments — building the confidence and competence needed for independent nursing practice.

  • 9 Weeks

    Medical-Surgical Nursing

    Supervised practice in medical and surgical wards — patient assessment, pre- and post-operative care, wound care, and medication administration.

  • 12 Weeks

    Midwifery & Gynecology

    The longest rotation — covering antenatal monitoring, delivery assistance, postnatal care, and gynecological nursing procedures.

  • 3 Weeks

    Pediatrics

    Child health nursing in pediatric wards — immunisation, developmental monitoring, nutritional support, and family-centred care.

  • 3 Weeks

    Community Health

    Field postings at PHCs and community health centres — health education, immunisation drives, maternal health outreach, and rural healthcare.

  • 2 Weeks

    Psychiatry

    Exposure to psychiatric nursing — therapeutic relationships, patient safety, mental health assessment, and rehabilitation support.
