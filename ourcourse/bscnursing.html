About the Programme

What is B.Sc Nursing?

The Bachelor of Science in Nursing (B.Sc. Nursing) is a four-year undergraduate programme approved by the Indian Nursing Council (INC) and State Nursing Council. This degree prepares students to become professional nurses, leaders, and healthcare providers equipped with strong clinical skills, evidence-based knowledge, and ethical values.

The course integrates theory, laboratory training, and extensive clinical exposure, ensuring graduates are competent to serve in hospitals, community healthcare, research, and education — both in India and abroad.

Programme Goals

Programme Objectives

Graduates of the B.Sc. Nursing programme at Noor College will be equipped to:

  • Deliver professional nursing care using scientific principles and evidence-based practices.
  • Function as clinicians, educators, and administrators in healthcare institutions.
  • Promote preventive, promotive, curative, and rehabilitative health services.
  • Apply leadership and management skills in clinical and community settings.
  • Engage in nursing research and evidence-based practice to improve patient outcomes.
  • Pursue higher education — M.Sc. Nursing, Nurse Practitioner programmes, and Ph.D.

Clinical Training

B.Sc. Nursing students at Noor College complete over 4,000 hours of supervised clinical practice across multiple specialties — including night duties, rotational postings, and a comprehensive supervised internship ensuring real-world readiness.

4,000+ Hours of supervised
clinical practice

Medical-Surgical Units

Critical care, emergency medicine, and general surgical ward nursing — pre- and post-operative care, medication management and patient monitoring.

Maternity & Obstetrics

Antenatal monitoring, conducting supervised deliveries, intranatal care, postnatal support and obstetric emergency management.

Pediatrics

Infant, child, and adolescent healthcare — neonatal care, immunisation, pediatric emergency, and family-centred nursing.

Psychiatry & Mental Health

Therapeutic communication, mental health assessment, patient safety planning, and rehabilitation nursing in psychiatric units.

Community Health Nursing

Rural postings, school health programmes, public health campaigns, maternal health outreach, and community-based interventions.

Night Duties & Rotations

Mandatory rotational night duties and ward postings to ensure comprehensive, round-the-clock clinical competence and confidence.

Assessment

Examination & Evaluation

Assessment combines continuous internal evaluation with university examinations, ensuring comprehensive evaluation of both theoretical knowledge and practical nursing competence across all four years.

Component Weightage Details
Internal Assessment 25% Assignments, seminars, clinical performance and viva
University Examination 75% Theory and practical examinations per RGUHS norms
Passing – Theory & Practicals 50% Minimum 50% required separately in theory and practicals
Theory Attendance 75% Minimum attendance required to appear for university exams
Clinical Attendance 100% Full clinical attendance is mandatory throughout the programme
Final Year

Internship Programme

The final year internship is a comprehensive, supervised training programme spanning six clinical departments. It is designed to consolidate all four years of learning into real, independent nursing practice — building graduate-level competence and professional confidence.

  • 12 Weeks

    Medical-Surgical Nursing

    Comprehensive surgical and medical ward postings — patient assessment, critical care, wound management, drug administration and post-operative recovery nursing.

  • 12 Weeks

    Midwifery & Gynecology

    Full antenatal, intranatal, and postnatal care — conducting supervised deliveries, managing obstetric emergencies, and gynecological nursing procedures.

  • 8 Weeks

    Pediatrics

    Infant, child and adolescent nursing — neonatal care, immunisation, growth monitoring, pediatric emergency, and family education.

  • 8 Weeks

    Community Health Nursing

    Field postings at PHCs, school health programmes, health camps, maternal health outreach, and rural community nursing.

  • 4 Weeks

    Psychiatry

    Mental health unit postings — therapeutic communication, patient safety, mental health assessment, and psychiatric rehabilitation.

  • 4 Weeks

    Nursing Management & Leadership

    Ward management, team leadership, healthcare administration, quality assurance, and professional nursing management practices.
